Beginners UI/UX Design

From zero to Figma —
in two weeks.

80% of students had never opened Figma before. By the end of the program, every single one had a complete app prototype they could show to anyone. The energy at the final presentation was electric.

  • ✓Students built complete app wireframes in Figma
  • ✓Conducted real user research interviews with classmates
  • ✓Presented final designs to parents and guests
  • ✓Left with a portfolio piece and new career interest

"I designed my first app and my parents couldn't believe it was made by me. I want to be a UI designer now."

— Aryan, Grade 9 · UI/UX Design Camp 2026

Gamers Lab · Board Game Design

They didn't just
play games — they made them.

Groups of 3–4 students designed original board games. The rules had to be clear enough for anyone to understand, the mechanics had to be balanced, and the experience had to be fun. That's a harder brief than most adults get at work.

  • ✓Designed original board game from blank paper to prototype
  • ✓Learned systems thinking through game mechanics
  • ✓Ran playtesting sessions and iterated based on feedback
  • ✓Presented finished games to families at showcase

"The Gamers Lab was the first time I saw my son genuinely excited to fail at something and try again. That's what learning should look like."

— Rajesh Kumar · Parent, Gamers Lab 2026

Makers Canvas · Mandala & Mosaic Art

Patience, patterns,
and pure creativity.

This was the program that surprised everyone the most. Students — including those who'd never considered themselves artistic — spent hours in deep, focused creative flow. Parents were stunned. Teachers asked if we could run it every term.

  • ✓Created complete Mandala artworks from scratch
  • ✓Built Mosaic pieces using colour theory and composition
  • ✓Developed focus, patience and mindful practice
  • ✓Exhibited finished artwork at end-of-program showcase

"My daughter has ADHD and she sat for 3 hours working on her Mandala without looking up once. I was in tears. This was exactly what she needed."

— Parent · Makers Canvas 2026

Project Abhay · Legal Literacy

Every student deserves
to know their rights.

Project Abhay tackled something no other program does — making the law feel personal, relevant, and empowering for young people. Students left not just knowing their rights, but feeling ownership over them for the first time.

  • ✓Learned fundamental rights and child protection laws
  • ✓Ran interactive mock courtroom sessions
  • ✓Discussed real case studies relevant to teenagers
  • ✓Created community awareness posters for their schools

"Project Abhay gave my students something no textbook has — the feeling that the law belongs to them. Every school should run this program."

— Ms. Divya Nair · Teacher, Project Abhay Observer 2026
