Programs & Curriculum

Six programs.
One unforgettable school year.

Every Maekido program is built around real outcomes — students make something, learn something, and leave with something they're proud of. Four are proven from Summer 2026. Two are launching to schools now.

🎨
UI/UX Design
Grades 6–12 ✓ Proven Summer 2026

Beginners UI/UX Design

Students with zero design experience learn to think like designers — researching users, sketching wireframes, and building interactive prototypes in Figma. By the end, they present a real app design to a live audience.

  • Learn design thinking & user research
  • Build wireframes and user flows
  • Create interactive Figma prototypes
  • Present at a final showcase event
  • Leave with a portfolio piece

Gamers Lab
Grades 4–9 ✓ Proven Summer 2026

Gamers Lab · Board Game Design

Students don't just play games — they make them. From concept to playable prototype, students design the mechanics, write the rules, and test their games with real players. This is systems thinking disguised as play.

  • Develop original board game concept
  • Design rules, mechanics and balance
  • Create physical game prototype
  • Playtest and iterate with peers
  • Showcase finished game to families
Grades 4–9In-SchoolAfter-School Club

Makers Canvas
Grades 4–10 ✓ Proven Summer 2026

Makers Canvas · Mandala & Mosaic Art

A deeply calming and creative program where students master two ancient art forms — Mandala drawing and Mosaic tiling. This program builds focus, patience, fine motor skills, and genuine artistic confidence in students who may never have thought of themselves as artists.

  • Complete multiple Mandala artworks
  • Create Mosaic pieces using colour theory
  • Develop focus, patience and mindfulness
  • Build artistic confidence and self-expression
  • Exhibit work at end-of-program showcase

Project Abhay
Grades 7–12 ✓ Proven Summer 2026

Project Abhay · Legal Literacy

Every student has rights — most don't know them. Project Abhay changes that. Students explore India's legal system through interactive sessions, mock courts, and real case studies that are directly relevant to their lives as young people.

  • Understand fundamental rights under the Indian Constitution
  • Learn child protection laws and POCSO
  • Run mock courtroom sessions
  • Discuss real cases relevant to teenagers
  • Create community awareness materials
Grades 7–12In-SchoolAfter-School Club

Design Thinking
Grades 5–12 Coming to Schools

Design Thinking

The method behind every great product, service, and solution — taught to students. Using the 5-stage design thinking framework, students tackle real problems from their school and community, building empathy, creative confidence, and structured problem-solving skills that apply everywhere.

  • Master the 5-stage design thinking process
  • Conduct empathy interviews with real users
  • Define problem statements and HMW questions
  • Prototype and test real solutions
  • Present solutions at final showcase
Grades 5–12In-SchoolAfter-School Club

Robotics
Grades 5–10 Coming to Schools

Robotics

Hands-on robotics where students build, program and test robots to solve real challenges. This program bridges the gap between abstract STEM concepts and physical making — giving students something they can actually hold, program, and watch come to life.

  • Build and assemble robots from components
  • Learn basic programming and logic
  • Complete challenge-based missions
  • Work in teams to solve engineering problems
  • Present final robot at showcase event

Beginners UI/UX Design

From zero to Figma —
in two weeks.

80% of students had never opened Figma before. By the end of the program, every single one had a complete app prototype they could show to anyone. The energy at the final presentation was electric.

  • ✓Students built complete app wireframes in Figma
  • ✓Conducted real user research interviews with classmates
  • ✓Presented final designs to parents and guests

"I designed my first app and my parents couldn't believe it was made by me. I want to be a UI designer now."

Gamers Lab · Board Game Design

They didn't just
play games — they made them.

Groups of 3–4 students designed original board games. The rules had to be clear enough for anyone to understand, the mechanics had to be balanced, and the experience had to be fun. That's a harder brief than most adults get at work.

  • ✓Designed original board game from blank paper to prototype
  • ✓Learned systems thinking through game mechanics
  • ✓Ran playtesting sessions and iterated based on feedback
  • ✓Presented finished games to families at showcase

"The Gamers Lab was the first time I saw my son genuinely excited to fail at something and try again. That's what learning should look like."

— Rajesh Kumar · Parent, Gamers Lab 2026

Our Story

We started because the gap
was too obvious to ignore.

Maekido was born from a simple observation: Indian schools produce some of the world's most hardworking students — but very few are taught to design, create, question, or make. We started with Summer programs to test our ideas with real kids. Summer 2026 proved we were onto something real.

"Every child is born a maker. Our job is to give them the tools, the language, and the confidence to prove it."

Vision

To be the place where India's next generation of designers, innovators, and leaders begin.

Mission

To make world-class creative education accessible, joyful, and career-relevant for every Indian student.

Tagline

Future Makers
Start Here.

Our Values

What drives everything we do

🎨
Design-First
We lead with creativity. Design thinking is the vehicle for every program we run, regardless of subject.
⚡
Action-Oriented
Students build real things — not just learn theory. Every session ends with something made, not just something memorised.
🌍
Impact-Aware
We connect making with meaning — social impact, empathy, and responsibility are baked into how we teach.
🤝
Community-Led
Peer learning, collaboration, and a culture where every student's work is celebrated equally.
